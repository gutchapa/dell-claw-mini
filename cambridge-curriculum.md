## What is the Cambridge Curriculum?

The Cambridge International Curriculum is the world's most popular international qualification for 5–14 year olds, taken in over **10,000 schools across 160 countries**. It emphasizes:

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Deep Subject Understanding** | Not rote memorization |
| **Critical Thinking & Problem Solving** | Applied learning, not textbook repetition |
| **Global Recognition** | Qualifications accepted by universities worldwide |
| **Flexible Progression** | Students move at their own pace with clear milestones |

## Assessment Approach

We don't believe in stressful exams for young children. Cambridge at Bodhana uses:

- **Cambridge Primary Progression Tests** — age-appropriate, low-stakes checkpoints
- **Montessori Portfolio Reviews** — teacher observations of hands-on work
- **Student-Led Conferences** — children present their own learning journey to parents
- **Cambridge Checkpoint (Grade 8)** — first formal diagnostic, used to personalize IGCSE prep

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Is Cambridge better than CBSE?
**A:** Neither is "better" — Cambridge is ideal for families considering higher education abroad or wanting an inquiry-based, international approach. CBSE is exam-centric and India-focused. Many of our Cambridge students later transition seamlessly to CBSE Grade 11 if needed.

### Q: Will my child struggle if we move to a CBSE school later?
**A:** No. Cambridge Primary covers the same concepts as CBSE but with deeper understanding. Our transition support includes bridge programs for families switching boards.

### Q: Do Cambridge students take board exams?
**A:** Cambridge Checkpoint (Grade 8) is diagnostic — not a pass/fail board exam. IGCSE (Grade 10) is the first formal qualification, recognized equivalently to CBSE/ICSE by Indian universities.

### Q: Is the Cambridge program only for NRI families?
**A:** Absolutely not. 70% of our Cambridge families are Chennai-based professionals who want global-ready education without leaving the city.
